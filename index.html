This proposal restructures the original low-estimate $47,500 custom MVP build into a lower-friction hybrid payment model: a reduced upfront payment, a monthly base rate, and a limited subscription revenue share that ends once the agreed cap is reached.

Recommended hybrid structure
$52,500 Cap

The client pays $15,000 upfront, then $4,500 per month, plus 10% of gross subscription revenue until GORGO Company / KORVA Systems has received $52,500 total compensation for the initial MVP build.

After the cap is paid, the revenue-share obligation ends. Ongoing support and operating expenses remain separate.

Why this structure works

  • Protects the original $47,500 project value while giving the client a reduced upfront commitment.
  • Adds only $5,000 above the original low estimate to account for deferred-payment risk.
  • Aligns part of compensation with subscription performance without making KORVA an unpaid technical cofounder.
  • Creates a clear end point: once $52,500 is paid, the subscription revenue share stops.

Subscription revenue share definition

Revenue share should be calculated from gross subscription revenue so it is simple to audit and not dependent on internal expense decisions.

Included revenue

  • Monthly subscriptions
  • Annual subscriptions
  • Premium upgrades and higher-tier plans
  • Discounted or promotional subscriptions
  • Bundled access when the app is part of a paid trading product
  • Any recurring access fee connected to the platform

When revenue share ends

Revenue share ends automatically once the combined total of the upfront payment, monthly base payments, and revenue-share payments reaches $52,500.

Important: after the cap is reached, new feature work, maintenance, data costs, infrastructure, and support are not included unless a separate agreement is signed.

Actual project tools and operating costs

These costs are shown separately so the client understands the difference between build compensation and the actual operating stack required to run the product.

Cost rule: third-party tools, data subscriptions, hosting, infrastructure, compliance, payment processing, and API usage are client-paid operating expenses. They are not included in the $52,500 compensation cap unless specifically agreed in writing.
Cost categoryHow it will be handledWhy it matters
Market data / trading data APIsClient pays actual provider costs directly or reimburses at cost.Trading apps often require paid data access, rate limits, commercial licensing, or real-time feed upgrades.
Hosting and databaseClient-owned account preferred. Costs billed directly to client.Keeps the app, database, and infrastructure under the client’s control.
Authentication and user accountsClient pays any platform-level account/auth charges if usage exceeds free or included tiers.Needed for subscriptions, protected access, customer accounts, and role-based features.
Payment processingStripe, Square, or equivalent processing fees are client expenses.Subscription payments create processor fees that should not reduce the development compensation cap.
AI / automation APIsUsage-based AI costs are billed to the client or paid through client-owned API accounts.AI costs can increase with user volume, automation depth, and request frequency.
Email, notifications, and analyticsClient pays vendor costs if used.Needed for onboarding emails, alerts, product analytics, and subscription operations.
Legal, compliance, disclosuresClient is responsible for professional review and required language.Trading-related products may need clear disclaimers, terms, risk language, and regulatory review.
Post-launch supportHandled under a separate monthly support agreement.The MVP build cap covers build delivery, not indefinite maintenance or support.
Client-friendly wording: final tool costs will be confirmed after vendor selection. No third-party subscription or data-feed cost will be represented as included unless it is expressly listed in the signed scope.
